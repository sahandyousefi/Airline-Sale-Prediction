## Methodology

The project follows a structured data science workflow:

**1. Data Collection and Understanding**
Exploration of the dataset including feature types, distributions, and initial quality assessment.

**2. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)**
Statistical summaries and visualizations to uncover patterns in booking behavior, temporal trends, price distributions, and demand drivers across airline routes and time periods.

**3. Data Preprocessing**
Handling of missing values, encoding of categorical variables, feature engineering from departure and booking dates, and normalization where applicable.

**4. Model Development**
Construction and training of predictive models including regression-based methods, ensemble methods such as Random Forest and Gradient Boosting, and classification models for binary outcomes such as buy/wait signals.

**5. Model Evaluation**
Performance assessment using metrics such as Mean Absolute Error (MAE), Root Mean Squared Error (RMSE), R-squared, and accuracy or F1-score for classification tasks.

**6. Interpretation and Insights**
Feature importance analysis and business-level recommendations derived from model outputs.

## Key Findings

The analysis identifies the primary factors influencing airline ticket sales, including departure timing, days-to-departure, route characteristics, and airline-specific pricing patterns. The final model demonstrates meaningful predictive performance on held-out data, with feature importance rankings that align with domain expectations in revenue management literature.
